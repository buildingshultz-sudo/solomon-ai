## Executive Summary

This plan details a focused, two-week marketing campaign to drive sales of the “Building What Matters” merch line ahead of Father’s Day. Leveraging authentic shop visuals and the unique “Tradesman meets AI” niche, the strategy combines storytelling, product education, and clear call-to-actions (CTAs) tailored for each channel.

### Goals
- **Increase merch sales** by at least 30% compared to previous 14-day periods.
- **Grow community engagement** across all social channels.
- **Strengthen brand identity** as the go-to for inspired, skill-driven dads and families.

### At-a-Glance: Key Components
- **Unified theme:** “Fatherhood is Building What Matters.”
- **Posting schedule:** Consistent, coordinated cross-platform strategy (daily content).
- **Shop visuals:** Authentic, in-action product photography & video teasers from Jed’s workshop.
- **Message angles:** Celebrate builders, honor dads, inspire skill-building across generations.
- **Measurement:** Track sales, engagement metrics, post reach, UTM-tagged conversions.

## 4. Platform-Specific Strategies

### YouTube

- **Shorts:** 20–30s “Dad in the Shop” and gift recommendation segments. Include merch visual, spoken CTA (“Link below”).
- **Main Channel Post:** Pin a comment with direct shop link, use description for UTM-tagged URL.
- **CTA Example:**  
  > “This Father’s Day, show dad you love what he builds. Check out the Building What Matters merch—link in description.”

### Instagram

- **Stories:** Daily countdown sticker starting June 6. Swipe-up linked directly to shop.
- **Feed/Carousel:** Mix shop shots with family/workshop moments. Use “Tag your dad” and “Share your project” prompts.
- **Reels:** Shop walkthrough, kid testimonials, “Why my dad deserves this” (short, emotionally driven).
- **Hashtags:** #BuildingWhatMatters #MakersDad #FathersDay2026 (see full list in Section 6).

### TikTok

- **Trends:** Sync “Shop With Me” and “Building with Dad” soundtracks if trending.
- **Short-Form:** 15–30s hands-on, funny, or emotional builds.  
- **CTA:** Always direct to link-in-bio; reinforce urgency week 2 (“Only X days left!”).

### Facebook (Both Pages)

- **Story and Feed Posts:** Use slightly different copy for “Irish Craftsman” (heritage/pride) vs. “Building Shultz” (modern/maker angle).
- **Group Posts:** If applicable, ask followers to share their own father/dad stories.
- **Pin Post:** Keep buy link prominent for duration of campaign.

### Email Newsletter

- **Subject Lines:**   
  - “Give Dad a Gift That Lasts—Built for Builders.”
  - “Don’t Miss: Father’s Day Shop”  
- **Body Copy:** Story-driven, featuring Jed or buyer highlights. Include hero image and 1-2 CTAs per email.

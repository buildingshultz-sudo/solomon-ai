- [Gumroad Search](https://www.gumroad.com/)  

### 3.2. Recommendations

- **Maintain $19 Base Price** for main ebook (matches dominant price anchor in niche, supported by market data)
- **Consider a Tiered “Plus” Version** ($27–29) with audio walk-through or exclusive prompt pack
- **No Free/PWYW** for lead magnet version—use free PDF excerpt as a funnel to email list instead
- **Launch Discount Option:** Consider a limited-time $15 offer for first 72 hours to drive urgency/velocity in early sales phase

### 3.3. Rationale

- $19–$29 is now the standard range for actionable, niche AI guides with real-world examples (not generic listicles)
- Lower prices (<$14) signal “blog post in PDF form” and reduce perceived value
- Bundling templates or bonus content justifies pricing at or above $20
- Scarcity/bonus (“first 100 buyers”) drives initial momentum

## 6. Actionable Recommendations

### 6.1. Positioning

- **Emphasize “Tradesman Tested”:** Focus every lead sentence on Jed’s credibility as a real, working builder and father. AI by the shop, for the shop—not “theory from an office.”
- **Outcome Over Information:** Each section, headline, and visual cues the *result*: “Build X faster,” “Price jobs with AI,” “Automate weekend projects.”
- **Non-Intimidating Onramp:** Remove jargon, give “easy wins” up front—make tradesmen feel this is doable *today*, not “one day.”

### 6.2. Sales/Content Funnel Moves

- **Collect Preorders / Waitlist**: If not ready to launch, swap “buy now” with “reserve your spot.”
- **Offer a Lead Magnet:** Free “Weekend with AI” mini-guide in exchange for email (not full ebook).
- **Community CTA:** Post-purchase, invite buyers to exclusive Facebook group or newsletter for follow-ups and testimonials.

### 6.3. Visuals

- **No robots, no ‘A.I. face’ stock art:** Tradesmen trust *hands and tools* — keep AI symbols subtle and backgrounded.
- **Highlight ‘Inside-the-Shop’ Practicality:** Muster images and language that reflect the *shop floor,* not just a ‘digital download.’

### 6.4. Review and Iterate

- **Monitor First 20–30 Sales Closely:** Track conversion, refund rate, and first reviews.
- **Solicit Feedback from Three Target Customers Before Public Launch:** Real tradesmen—iterate language and promises.

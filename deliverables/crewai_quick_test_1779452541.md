## Development Framework

### 1. Workshop Readiness
- **Layout Optimization:** Map current tool placement, workflow paths, and storage. Reorganize to minimize movement between rough milling, joinery, assembly, and finishing zones.
- **Tool Maintenance Cycle:** Create a quarterly inspection log covering blade sharpening, dust collection performance, lubrication, and calibration. Capture before/after metrics (e.g., cut accuracy) for future content.
- **Material Inventory Tracker:** Maintain a spreadsheet of hardwood, softwood, reclaimed lumber, sheet goods, and specialty items. Include usage history to anticipate replenishment before projects stall.
- **Safety Systems:** Standardize PPE placement, emergency procedures, and signage. Document for potential workshop safety mini-course.

### 2. Curriculum for Continuous Improvement
- **Skill Blocks:** Dedicate monthly focus themes (e.g., mortise-and-tenon precision, veneer application, hand-tool joinery).
- **Micro-Challenges:** Set weekly exercises that can be filmed quickly—e.g., dovetail race, cutting board alignment test, finish comparison test.
- **Peer Benchmarking:** Review two master craftsman videos each month, extracting one technique to replicate or adapt.
- **AI Integration:** Use IronEdit prototypes or existing AI tools to catalog build steps, auto-tag footage, and create searchable process libraries.

### 3. Content and Product Alignment
- **Project Selection Matrix:** Rate ideas by storytelling potential, educational value, monetization pathways, and material accessibility.
- **Documentation Standards:** Ensure every build includes robust B-roll, voiceover notes, material lists, and cutting diagrams to repurpose into digital downloads.
- **Signature Series:** Develop recurring formats (e.g., “Reclaimed to Refined,” “Weekend Builds with the Kids,” “Tool-less Challenges”) to keep the woodworking narrative consistent.

## Actionable Recommendations

### Workshop Infrastructure
- **Week 1–2:** Perform a full shop audit, take measurements, sketch current vs. ideal workflows. Deliverable: annotated floor plan and punch list.
- **Week 3:** Implement quick wins (reposition clamps, label storage, add mobile bases where needed). Film before/after for content.
- **Month 2:** Install a dedicated lighting rig and overhead mic to improve footage quality without slowing builds.
- **Month 3:** Introduce a tool calibration log accessible via tablet or QR code on the tool cabinet.

### Skill Training Cadence
- **Weekly Practice (2-hour blocks):** One evening session dedicated purely to technique drills, not project work. Example: test-fit joinery on scrap until gaps are <0.5 mm.
- **Monthly Masterclass:** Choose a single advanced technique, produce a mini internal report documenting the steps, lessons learned, and filming angles.
- **Quarterly Capstone Build:** Commit to one flagship piece every quarter (e.g., dining table, credenza) that can anchor a multi-video arc and support a premium plan release.

### Monetization & Content Integration
- **Digital Plan Pipeline:** Convert each flagship build into a downloadable plan, complete with cut lists, 3D renders, and optional AI-generated variants (different dimensions or styles).
- **Workshop Livestreams:** Host quarterly live Q&As or build-alongs to drive community engagement and test premium membership interest.
- **Affiliate Partnerships:** Build relationships with tool brands aligned with Jed’s authenticity. Prepare a woodworking-specific press kit highlighting audience demographics, engagement metrics, and past build outcomes.
- **Educational Products:** Expand Builders AI Blueprint with woodworking-specific AI workflows (e.g., AI prompt templates for jig ideas, finish schedules, or material optimization).

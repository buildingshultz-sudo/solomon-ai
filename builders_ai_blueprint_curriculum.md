# The Builder's AI Blueprint — Course Curriculum
**By Jedidiah Shultz**
*Direct, practical, no fluff. Built for tradesmen, contractors, and makers who'd rather be on the tools than behind a screen.*

---

## How This Course Works

I'm not a tech guy. I'm a journeyman pipefitter from Indiana who got tired of losing his evenings and weekends to paperwork. This course is the system I built to get that time back, laid out the way I'd teach a new apprentice — one step at a time, no jargon, nothing you can't use on your very next job.

Seven modules. Each one stands on its own, but they build in order. Every module has short video lessons and a one-page worksheet you actually fill out with a real job — not busywork. You don't need to be good with computers. You need a phone or a laptop, a free AI tool, and the willingness to try one prompt. That's it.

**The rule for the whole course:** The AI is the navigator. You are the captain. It does the heavy lifting; you check the math and make the call. Be Inspired, Stay Humble, and Build.

## Module 1: Meet Your Digital Apprentice (Foundations)

**Learning objective:** By the end of this module, a tradesman who has never touched AI will be able to open a free AI tool, understand in plain terms what it is and isn't, and write a clear instruction that gets a useful answer instead of garbage. We strip out every buzzword and treat AI like what it really is — a brand-new apprentice who is fast and tireless but needs to be told exactly who they are, what the job is, and what "done" looks like. You'll finish this module comfortable enough to stop being intimidated and start putting the tool to work.

### Video Lessons
1. **What AI Actually Is (In Boots-On Terms).** Forget the Silicon Valley talk — AI is a tool that reads and writes language fast, like having a sharp office assistant who never sleeps. We cover what it's genuinely good at (writing, math, planning, explaining) and where it'll let you down so you never get burned.
2. **Picking Your Tool and Getting Signed In.** A no-stress walkthrough of getting set up on ChatGPT or Claude on your phone and your computer. We keep it to the free version and show you the one screen you'll actually use — the chat box.
3. **The Apprentice Rule: Why Most Guys Get Garbage.** The biggest mistake is treating AI like Google and typing "how much wood do I need." You'll learn the three things every good instruction needs — a role, the details, and the result you want — so the tool answers like a 30-year pro instead of a stranger.
4. **Your First Prompt, Live.** We type a real prompt together, hit enter, and read the answer out loud so you see exactly how it works. No theory — by the end of this lesson you'll have made the AI do something useful with your own two thumbs.
5. **You're the Captain: Checking the Work.** The AI is confident even when it's wrong, so you never paste-and-pray. We show you how to spot-check numbers, push back ("you missed the waste on the miters"), and make the tool fix itself.
6. **Keeping It Private and Professional.** Quick, plain rules on what not to paste (customer bank info, anything you wouldn't text a buddy) and how to keep your business details safe while still getting great answers.

### Companion Worksheet: "First Day on the Job"
1. Write down the three business tasks that eat your evenings the most (estimates, scheduling, chasing payments, etc.) — these are the jobs you'll hand your apprentice first.
2. Sign in to one AI tool and paste the starter prompt provided; record how long the answer took versus how long it'd take you by hand.
3. Take one answer the AI gave you and circle one thing it got wrong or left out — prove to yourself you're still the captain.
4. Rewrite a lazy prompt ("help me with my deck") into a proper one using the Role + Details + Result formula.
5. List one task you will NOT trust AI with yet, and why — drawing your own line builds the right habits early.

## Module 2: The Job Site Planner — Material & Labor Logic

**Learning objective:** This module turns AI into your personal estimator and scheduler so planning a job goes from an hour at the kitchen table to about three minutes. You'll learn the "Master Carpenter" workflow: feeding the tool your real project details and getting back an accurate material takeoff, a waste factor that accounts for your specific layout, and a phased timeline you can hand to your crew. By the end you'll plan jobs with confidence, order materials once, and stop losing money to bad guesses and emergency runs to the supply house.

### Video Lessons
1. **The Master Carpenter Prompt.** We build the prompt that gives the AI a role, the square footage, the exact materials, and the tricky details (like a picture-frame border) that change the math. This one prompt is the backbone of every job you'll ever plan.
2. **Waste Factors That Actually Match Your Layout.** Learn why a standard horizontal deck might need 10% but a herringbone or mitered border needs 15%, and how to make the AI justify the number. You'll never eat the cost of over-ordering or stall a job under-ordering again.
3. **Phased Timelines for a Real Crew.** Turn a pile of plans into a clean three-phase schedule — prep, rough-in, finish — sized to how many guys you actually have. You'll walk away with a timeline you can text to your crew and your client.
4. **The "What Went Wrong" Follow-Up.** Your second guy calls in sick and rain's in the forecast — instead of ripping up the schedule, you just tell the AI and it recalculates. We practice adjustment prompts so your plan survives contact with the real world.
5. **The Cut-List Optimizer (Metal & Wood).** For welders and woodworkers: feed the AI your cuts and stock lengths and it tells you which cuts to make on which stick to leave the least scrap. That saved material is pure profit going straight back in your pocket.
6. **Verifying the Takeoff Before You Buy.** A short discipline lesson on reading the breakdown line by line and catching the one number that's off before it becomes a problem. The AI gets you 90% there in three minutes; this is how you handle the last 10%.

### Companion Worksheet: "Plan Your Next Real Job"
1. Pick a job you have coming up and write out its dimensions, primary materials, and crew size in one place.
2. Run the Master Carpenter prompt on it and paste in the material takeoff the AI gives you.
3. Record the waste factor it recommended and one sentence on WHY it chose that number for your layout.
4. Run one adjustment prompt (lost a crew member, weather day, rush deadline) and note how the timeline changed.
5. Compare the AI's takeoff to what you'd have ordered by gut — write down the difference in dollars or trips saved.

## Module 3: The Client Closer — Professionalism & Invoices

**Learning objective:** This module gives you a professional secretary who knows exactly how to say things, so communication stops costing you money. You'll learn to turn rough, frustrated thoughts into polished estimates that explain the "why" behind your price, and to chase down late payments firmly without burning bridges. By the end you'll close more high-end bids because clients understand your value, and you'll collect what you're owed without the uncomfortable blank-screen stare.

### Video Lessons
1. **Explaining the "Why" Behind Your Price.** Paste a rough estimate and have the AI write the paragraph that turns a price objection into a selling point — like why Schluter waterproofing that costs more never leaks. You'll make the client see they're buying quality and longevity, not just paying more.
2. **The Estimate Rewrite.** Take your bullet-point notes and let the AI build them into a clean, professional proposal that makes a one-man shop look like a top-tier outfit. Same work, same price — but now it reads like you've got an office staff.
3. **Collecting Late Payments Without the Fight.** We write the "polite but firm" email for a client who's two weeks late on an invoice, the kind that gets results without sounding angry. You'll keep a script on hand so chasing money never ruins your week again.
4. **Handling the Tough Conversations.** Scope creep, change orders, and the "can you just throw this in for free?" ask — we draft responses that hold your ground and stay professional. You protect your margin without becoming the bad guy.
5. **Setting Your Tone Once.** Teach the AI how you talk — straight, respectful, no corporate fluff — so every message sounds like you and not a robot. Get this dialed in and every future email starts halfway done.

### Companion Worksheet: "Get Paid What You're Worth"
1. Take a real bid you're nervous to send and list the premium materials or methods you're using and why they matter.
2. Run the "explain the why" prompt and paste the professional paragraph it writes for your proposal.
3. Draft a late-payment email for a real (or recent) overdue invoice and rate how it sounds — firm but not angry.
4. Write down one client conversation you've been avoiding, and have the AI draft your opening message.
5. Define your communication tone in three words and save a reusable instruction the AI can apply to every email.

## Module 4: The Storyteller — Content & Marketing

**Learning objective:** This module turns the photos and videos rotting on your phone into marketing that actually books work, without you becoming a "social media person." You'll learn to hand the AI your raw, messy shop notes and get back scripts, captions, and posts that make people want to hire you. By the end you'll have a repeatable way to show your work online so the good jobs come to you instead of relying on word-of-mouth until it dries up.

### Video Lessons
1. **Shop Notes to 60-Second Script.** Jot five quick bullets about your day and watch the AI build a video script with a hook, a middle, and a call to action. You set up the phone, read it, and post — the whole "marketing" job takes five minutes.
2. **Finding the Story in the Build.** Learn how the AI pulls the interesting angle out of an ordinary job — like the bullet you found buried in 100-year-old barn wood — and leads with it. Every project has a hook; this teaches you to spot it.
3. **One Build, Every Platform.** Take a single project and spin it into an Instagram Reel script, a YouTube description, and a Facebook post, each in the right tone and length. Post once, show up everywhere, with almost no extra work.
4. **Writing Captions and Hooks That Stop the Scroll.** We generate punchy openers and captions that grab a thumb mid-scroll instead of getting ignored. You'll learn to ask the AI for three options and pick the one that sounds most like you.
5. **Batch a Week of Content in One Sitting.** Use slow-day downtime to turn a handful of recent jobs into a week's worth of posts at once. Consistency is what grows a page, and batching is how busy guys stay consistent.

### Companion Worksheet: "Show Your Work"
1. Write five rough bullet points about a recent job — what you built, a problem you solved, anything unusual.
2. Run the shop-notes-to-script prompt and paste the 60-second script it produces.
3. Take that one job and generate three versions: an Instagram caption, a YouTube description, and a Facebook post.
4. Ask the AI for three different hooks for the same project and circle the one that sounds the most like you.
5. Plan a one-sitting batch: list three recent jobs you could turn into a week of content this weekend.

## Module 5: The Family Foundation — Time Management

**Learning objective:** This is the heart of the whole course: using AI as a personal scheduler that puts boundaries around your work so it stops bleeding into your life. You'll learn to build realistic, time-blocked days that get the shop work AND the paperwork done while fiercely protecting dinner, your kid's ballgame, and your weekend. By the end you'll prove to yourself that you can run the business without sacrificing the family you're running it for.

### Video Lessons
1. **The Protected-Weekend Schedule.** Build a Saturday that fits four hours of shop time, a run to baseball practice, and a 6 PM family dinner — without anything getting dropped. The AI has no emotions, so it blocks your day objectively and tells you when to put the tools down.
2. **The Honest To-Do Triage.** Dump your whole overwhelming list in and ask the AI which three things are actually urgent and which can wait until Monday. You stop treating everything as a fire and start working the list that matters.
3. **Time-Blocking a Brutal Week.** Lay a heavy job week over your real life and let the AI carve out where quotes, supply runs, and admin actually fit. You'll see your week before it happens instead of getting steamrolled by it.
4. **Building in Buffers for Real Life.** Learn to tell the AI about the rain, the sick kid, and the supply-house detour so your schedule survives the day. A plan that ignores reality is just a wish; this makes yours hold up.
5. **The Shutdown Routine.** Set a hard stop and a short end-of-day ritual so work actually ends and you're present at the dinner table. We script the boundary so the business doesn't follow you into the living room.

### Companion Worksheet: "Get Your Evenings Back"
1. Write down your actual wake-up time, hard family commitments, and the shop tasks you must finish this weekend.
2. Run the protected-weekend prompt and paste the time-blocked schedule it builds.
3. Dump your current to-do list into the AI and record the three tasks it flagged as genuinely urgent.
4. Identify one recurring "real life" disruption (weather, kids, travel) and write a buffer instruction for it.
5. Write your personal hard-stop time and the three-step shutdown routine you'll run every workday.

## Module 6: Steering the Ship — Business Strategy

**Learning objective:** This module gives you the business consultant you could never afford, working for free off the numbers from your own jobs. You'll learn to feed the AI what you charged, what materials cost, and the hours a job actually took, and have it find exactly where your profit is leaking. By the end you'll set an hourly rate that covers your real costs, quote with a buffer that protects your margin, and stop working hard just to break even.

### Video Lessons
1. **The Profit-Leak Audit.** Hand the AI the numbers from your last three jobs and let it compare what you estimated to what actually happened. It pinpoints the leaks — the labor you underbid, the rental you forgot — and tells you how to plug them.
2. **Why You Keep Underbidding Labor.** Most guys underestimate labor by about 25%; we use the AI to find your personal pattern and build a buffer into your next quote. You bid off data instead of optimism.
3. **Setting an Hourly Rate That Pays You.** Plug in your overhead, the take-home you want, and your billable hours, and the AI does the math for your true minimum rate. You'll never pull a number out of thin air or undercharge again.
4. **Building Your Pre-Bid Checklist.** Have the AI turn your past mistakes into a standard checklist — tool rentals, dump fees, drive time — that you run before every bid. The stuff that ate your margin last year stops slipping through.
5. **Reading Your Numbers Like a Captain.** A short lesson on asking the AI plain questions about your jobs ("which type of work makes me the most per hour?") so you steer toward profitable work. You start running the business instead of letting it run you.

### Companion Worksheet: "Audit Your Own Books"
1. Gather the basic numbers from your last three jobs: amount charged, material cost, estimated hours, actual hours, and any surprise expenses.
2. Run the profit-leak audit prompt and write down the single biggest leak it found.
3. Calculate your true minimum hourly rate using the overhead + take-home + billable-hours prompt.
4. Build a pre-bid checklist from your own past misses and list the five items you'll never forget again.
5. Ask the AI which of your job types is most profitable per hour and write down one decision you'll make because of the answer.

## Module 7: Building Your System (Capstone)

**Learning objective:** This final module keeps you from the most common failure — trying to do everything at once, getting overwhelmed, and quitting. You'll pick one prompt, win with it this week, and then build a simple personal prompt library and a 30-day rollout so AI becomes a permanent part of how you run your shop. By the end you'll have a sustainable system, not a one-time experiment, and a clear plan for getting your time back for good.

### Video Lessons
1. **Pick One and Win This Week.** Walking into AI is like walking into a tool store — you want it all but only have two hands. We help you choose the single prompt that solves your most painful problem right now and commit to using it before anything else.
2. **Build Your Prompt Library.** Save the prompts that worked into a simple notes file so you never start from a blank screen again. We show you how to organize them by job so the right one is always two taps away.
3. **Your 30-Day Rollout.** Lay out a month where you add one new habit per week — planning, then client comms, then content, then numbers — so it sticks instead of overwhelming you. Slow is smooth, and smooth is permanent.
4. **Making It Yours.** Teach the AI your trade, your voice, and your standards once, and reuse that setup everywhere. The more it sounds like you, the more you'll actually use it.
5. **Knowing the Line.** A closing lesson on what to keep doing by hand and where AI earns its keep, so you stay the craftsman and the captain. The goal was never to turn you into a programmer — it's to get your time back for the shop and the dinner table.

### Companion Worksheet: "Your 30-Day Plan"
1. Choose the ONE prompt from this course you'll use first and write the exact job you'll use it on this week.
2. Start your prompt library: copy in the three prompts that have worked best for you so far.
3. Map a 30-day rollout — write which module's habit you'll focus on in each of the next four weeks.
4. Write your reusable "about me" setup (your trade, crew size, voice, standards) that you'll paste into the AI going forward.
5. Draw your line: list two tasks you'll always keep hands-on and two you're now handing to your digital apprentice for good.
